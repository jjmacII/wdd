Susie Q Gourds

Creating works of art with love.

My name is Susan Davis but a lot of people know me as Susieq. I have been doing gourd art for more than 10 years. Art has always been one of my passions and I am a self-taught artist. After an elderly lady gave me a gourd to work on I fell in love with the gourd. Using it as my pallet I began experimenting with different mediums from weaving, painting, wood burning and many more mediums. I love being creative doing one of a kind art. I am well known in western North Carolina and you can see more of my work through out galleries in North Carolina. I also teach classes for local schools and in private. If you would like to schedule a time for me to teach a class please email or call me for available times. I thank God for giving me the ability to please people with my art. It makes me feel great when I hear them say, That's a Gourd?

You can also read a recent article that was published by the Mountain Laurel here

Before you can start the process of creating something beautiful out of the gourd, you first need to choose the type of gourd you want to work on and then clean it. Gourds are grown on the ground and need to be dried before you can begin working on them. It can take a year or more to dry a gourd. Once the gourd is dry and looks rotten then you need to scrub it down with soap and water so you have a clean surface to work on. After cleaning the gourd and letting it dry then you can begin your design process. Gourds can be cut, dyed, or painted on. If you can imagine it then it can be created on a gourd. After I am satisfied with how the piece looks I use a clear sealant to help protect the paint.
